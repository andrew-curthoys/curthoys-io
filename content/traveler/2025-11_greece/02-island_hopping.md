<h4 class="article-subheader">Your Ferry Will Depart From Gate E9</h4>
"Ok great! And where is E9?"

"That way," the Blue Star Ferries ticket agent points with the enthusiasm of
of a student twirling her hair with a pencil in an 80s movie.

"THAT WAY!!!" I shout to our family gathered at the crosswalk 200 yards away.
Unfortunately I am at the obtuse angle of the triangle formed between them, me
and "that way".

"I still need to see 3 more passports," the gate agent smarmily remarks.

*ohh for fucks sake* I mutter as I sprint towards our gaggle of familial
waddlers.

Three minutes later I return to the kiosk with three passports sheathed in their
own uniquely frustrating cloaks.

*"Here"* I mutter between my labored breathing as I slam the passports down on
the metal countertop.

"I'm sorry, we are too close to the ferry departure time to book you from this
office. You must book your ticket at the desk next to the departure terminal."

"And that's..."

"That way"

"*Thank you so much for your help*" I sardonically retort as I gather up all the
passports like an elementary school trip chaperone. I wheel around to meet our
family sidling up to the kiosk.

"Where to, nephew?"

"Gate E9"

"Ok and that's..."

"That way"

So we set off in the direction of *that way* with a tight timetable and a few
______ challenged passengers. After speedwalking a few hundred yards *that way*
the dry land abruptly ends with with no signs of gate E9. The only way to go,
other than plunging into the Aegean, is left. So I bear to port with engines at
full throttle. After another few hundred yards we reach another dead end. I
look around to see a sight for Gate E8.

*where the hell is E9???* 

I see a person walking down the pier in what looks to be an official capacity,
"excuse me, where is gate E9?"

"That way"

Of course it is. In this instance *that way* is another left from where we are,
which if you know anything about geometry, you know that two 90 degree lefts
send you back in the direction from whence you came. But at this point I'm too
flustered to think about anything other than getting on this damn boat. I am
project lead for this expedition and the guilt of missing the ferry is already
weighing on me.

Without gate E9 even in sight I drop my bags with cousin Laura and take off at
a jog with a handful of passports in need of companions in the form of ferry
passage documents.

I ramble past nearly half a mile of docks, slips, ferries, ships, and Greek
dockworkers before I reach gate E9 and the boat-side kiosk for Blue Star Ferries.

"*Yassas, I need 3 tickets for the ________ (time) ferry.*" I breathlessly tell
the ticket clerk.

"Oh, cutting it a little close aren't we?"

"I wasn't expecting a 2 km hike to get here from the ticket office."

"We recommend arriving to the gate 30 minutes before departure."

"*I'm aware*."

"Are the 3 passengers with you?"

"They're *coming*," I point from the arrow end of *that way* towards our family
making good time, all things considered.

"I see," she says as she click-clacks away at her keyboard in a way that only
ticket agents seem to do. "Ok, here are your tickets, the ferry will depart in
5 minutes from gate E9 directly behind you. *Enjoy your trip*."

"Efcharisto"(sp?)

The family arrives just in time to walk right up the gangplank.
