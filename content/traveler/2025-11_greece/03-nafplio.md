We sleep in before heading out to explore the town on foot. First order of
business is breakfast, so we make a 10 minute walk through the windy, narrow
streets of old Nafplio to Kalimera, a
recommendation from the bubbly receptionist at the hotel. We are seated on their
street-front patio. I don't tarry too long with the menu - the first item listed
is "Traditional Greek Breakfast" - when in Nafplio as they say.

The Traditional Greek Breakfast consists of:

* Greek single coffee with sweet spoon or mountain tea
* Freshly squeezed orange juice
* Crusty country bread with homemade jams, honey and butter
* Traditional kayianas
* Cheese bread with feta cheese and sesame seeds (served with grated tomato and olives)

"Let's grab a drink while we find a hotel to stay at tonight."

"Sounds like a great idea."

We wind through the narrow streets of Nafplio until we stumble into Μαύρες Γάτες
(The Black Cat), a low-key bar occupying the ground floor of a three-story building
with exposed stone walls behind a backlit bar. We find a spot on the covered
patio facing the street enclosed in temporary vinyl paneling presumably to keep
the warmth in but whose main function currently seems to be to keep the
cigarette smoke in.

The waiter comes around and we order a couple beers to accompany our hotel
search. I pull out my phone and the initial search turns up what must be 100
accommodation options. After a few price and location filters we whittle it down
to a handful of viable candidates. We scroll through half a beer's worth of
photos, hotel descriptions, and amenity lists.

After weighing the merits of the lodgings we settle on the King Othon Boutique
Hotel and a few taps later we have a room. The waiter comes around again and we
replenish our glasses. As the swill hits my lips I'm struck with a realization.
It's getting close to 10 PM and our hotel might actually have a closing time.

I set down my beer and say to Shannon, "hey I think I should run up to the hotel
real quick just to check in & grab the key in case. Just in case we don't get
locked out."
