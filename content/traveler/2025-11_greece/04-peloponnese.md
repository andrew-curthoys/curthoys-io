We reach Voidokilia Beach just before sunset. I don my brand new blue & white
striped zipup hoodie and step into the gentle wind that feels like it's just
about to get a little bite. It's a short walk to the beach, around a row of
scrubby trees and dunes. As we round the corner I nearly trip over my jaw as
the full extent of the beach comes into view.

A perfect crescent of pillowy ecru sand trace a graceful arc between towering
bookends of limestone guarding the entrance to the bay like hulking bodyguards
at the hottest club in town. As we walk down the fluffy beach Helios guides his
golden chariot beneath the crest of the southerly crag.

I wrap my arms around Shannon and embrace the beauty of the moment.
"I bet we can get a few more minutes of sunlight up on that ridge," I
remark, pointing to the smaller of the ridges to the north.

"Oh yeah great idea, we should grab the wine and snacks too"

"Talk about a great idea!!"

The trail leading to the ridge is a stone's throw from the car, where we grab
the wine, snacks, and a blanket to sit on. As we hike up the ridge we pass
the ruins of a Bronze Age Mycenaean (1680–1060 BC) tholos tomb, all of which is
left is a waist-high circle of stacked stones that made up the outer walls of
the tomb. It was excavated in the 1960s by Greek archeologist Spyridon Marinatos,
who, according to Wikipedia, "fancifully" named it the "Tomb of Thrasymedes"

*Tale of Thrasymedes*

We navigate through a narrow passage of thorny brush to emerge onto a rocky
promontory overlooking the Ionian Sea. We climb over striated benches of weathered
limestone to find a nice soft sandy spot between the rocks to spread our blanket.
We uncork the wine and assemble the assorted snacks and chocolates.

From our elevated perch we watch the sun dip down below the horizon once again,
this time for good, sinking below Poseidon's watery domain.

As much as I want to stay and gaze at the stars late into the night with my
sweetie, an armada of mosquitos has mustered with the setting of the sun and
have begun treating us like pin cushions. Unfortunately there's no escaping the
inevitability of mosquitos, having a cosmopolitan distribution, which I just 
learned means they occur in every land region on Earth. Yikes.

We reluctantly pack up camp and head back to the car. By this point the dusky
light on the horizon has faded into a nocturnal darkness. We step through the
thicket of thorny shrubs guided by our phone lights and take the path headed to
the left. Which is the way
we came... Right? We stumble through the darkness ducking branches and brambles
until we reach a dead end. Well I guess that wasn't the right way.

We retrace our steps and take the other fork we originally came to... Only to lead
to another dead end. We about face, and try another path. Or was this the first
path? More branches, more high stepping, more dead ends. We are wandering around
aimlessly in the dark.

Our original plan was to stay in Kalimata tonight, but I'm not too keen on
driving 2 hours in the dark, plus this beach is too incredible to leave. We must
come back tomorrow. I grab my phone to find accommodations for the night and
settle on the Three Charities Resort, a 10 minute drive away in Gialova.
