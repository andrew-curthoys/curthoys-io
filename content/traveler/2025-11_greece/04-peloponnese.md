We reach Voidokilia Beach just before sunset. I don my brand new blue & white
striped zipup hoodie and step into the gentle wind that feels like it's just
about to get a little bite. It's a short walk to the beach, around a row of
scrubby trees and dunes. As we round the corner I nearly trip over my jaw as
the full extent of the beach comes into view.

A perfect crescent of pillowy ecru sand trace a graceful arc between towering
bookends of limestone guarding the entrance to the bay like hulking bodyguards
at the hottest club in town. As we walk down the fluffy beach Helios guides his
golden chariot beneath the crest of the southerly crag.

I wrap my arms around Shannon and embrace the beauty of the moment.
"I bet we can get a few more minutes of sunlight up on that ridge," I
remark, pointing to the smaller of the ridges to the north.

"Oh yeah great idea, we should grab the wine and snacks too"

"Talk about a great idea!!"

The trail leading to the ridge is a stone's throw from the car, where we grab
the wine, snacks, and a blanket to sit on. As we hike up the ridge we pass
the ruins of a Bronze Age Mycenaean (1680–1060 BC) tholos tomb, all of which is
left is a waist-high circle of stacked stones that made up the outer walls of
the bomb. It was excavated in the 1960s by Greek archeologist Spyridon Marinatos,
who, according to Wikipedia, "fancifully" named it the "Tomb of Thrasymedes"
